Rules and Regulations

Academic Conduct:

1. Attendance:
- Students are required to maintain a minimum attendance percentage as per the college's policy. - Late entries and unauthorized leaves are subject to disciplinary actions.

2. Examinations:
- Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or academic dishonesty is strictly prohibited. - Electronic devices are not allowed during examinations unless explicitly permitted.


Code of Conduct:

3. Behavior: - Respectful behavior towards faculty, staff, and fellow students is mandatory. - Harassment, bullying, or any form of discrimination will not be tolerated.
4. Dress Code: - Students are expected to adhere to a specified dress code during college hours and events.


Campus Facilities:

5. Library Usage: - Maintain silence and order in the library. - Handle library resources with care, and return borrowed items on time.
6. Computer Labs: - Follow ethical use of computers and software. - No unauthorized software installations or tampering with equipment.


Health and Safety:

7. Drug and Alcohol: - The consumption or possession of illegal substances on the campus is strictly prohibited.
8. Safety Measures: - Adhere to safety guidelines in laboratories and workshops. - Report any hazards or unsafe conditions promptly.


Extracurricular Activities:

9. Student Organizations: - Students are encouraged to participate in clubs and organizations while maintaining academic priorities.
10. Events and Gatherings: - Organize and participate in college events responsibly and adhere to event guidelines.


Disciplinary Actions:

11. Violations: - Violations of these rules and regulations may result in disciplinary actions, including warnings, fines, suspension, or expulsion.
12. Appeals: - Students have the right to appeal disciplinary actions. Follow the prescribed appeal procedure.

Please remember to review and adapt these rules according to the specific needs, culture, and policies of SPIT or any other institution. Additionally, it's advisable to communicate these rules clearly to all students and ensure that they are aware of the consequences of non-compliance.
